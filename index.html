Furlough

Thursday 2nd April

Its been a while since I last posted, this has been because have had a lot to do at with work. I found out on Monday that I will be furloughed from my job, this means that I will not be needed to work and will be paid only 80% of my wages from the government.

I know I'm not the only one having this done as a lot of people around the world are, but to me this felt crap! Half of me thought great, I get to spend more time with my wife and daughter, our outgoings are less now so 80% should be fine, even though my wife comes off maternity pay this month. The other half felt like I was being fired, like I wouldn't have a job to go back to.. I wouln't be able to support my wife and child, I was quite worried.

Fortunately, due to some responsibilities that I have at Aggregate, my furlough does not start until the beggining of May, and I am lucky to have one more month of full pay before it drops. In my head that also means that maybe in a months time things might change and employees might start returning..we shall see.

Lara has started to do online classes to earn a few extra pennies, this is great for us and more importantly her, gives her something to do whilst in isolation, and also continue her business, which has been affected.

Its worrying times for all and hope that everyone is okay...

My trip to Tesco's

Monday 30th March

Yesterday, I had to make an essential trip to Tesco's to collect food. I was initially happy abou this as not been out the house much and nice to get out..further than our estate, I later regretted this...

My drive there, I was happy to see that the roads were quiet, seems that more are taking the advice and staying at home. I arrived at Tesco and could see that they had setup a one way in/out system, so walked in and grabbed a basket. It was at this point I checked the shopping list that Lara had wanted me to get and the list was as long as my arm. I then thought best get a trolley, but as I don't carry cash around with me, I didn't have a coin to use to get one! So there I go, two baskets one in each hand and having to stop every few minutes to check the shopping list, my arms getting longer and longer as the baskets got heavier. What a palava!

Now, I give it to Tesco, they had marked on the ground two meter marks, they had a lot of stock as were enforcing an only three of an item policy, and I was able to get quite a lot of my list, including the gluten free items for my wife. People were avoiding me.. I'm used to that anyway, but everyone was being civil and happy, it's nice to see in such situation we are all in.

I made it to the till with my two baskets, lines on the ground ensuring each customer kept their distance, my stuff scanned and processed, Â£70!! hadn't realised how much I had got. packed my things into bags and out to my car. Lara did insist on wiping down each product when I got home as was immediately told to go shower and put my clothes in the wash

It wasn't a great experience but essential to ensure have food in the house.

Clap for the NHS

Friday 27th March

We all joined in the 'Clap for NHS workers' last night, even Luna did! It was great to hear all joining in around us and definitely needed as they are doing such an amazing and tough job. I checked Facebook after and seeing peoples videos of all joining in across the country was definitely heart warming. We should do this every week to help show our support and lift our spirits!

I also think its amazing of those who have volunteered in the past few days, either doing over the phone check in's on isolated patients, distributing medication to vunerable people...simply awesome people!

Importance of Excercise

Thursday 26th March

It is important that during our time of isolation that we still find time to exercise and remain active, I find this more important as each day goes by.

Having a second job as a group exercise instructor, and finding due to the gym closures that am no longer teaching my weekly classes, my exercise level has gone down. I am finding myself sitting down at my desk throughout the day and then sitting down on the couch in the evenings.. not much exercise there hey! It is now that I find myself finishing work at 5pm, and then going out for a walk with my wife and daughter, not only that but take regular walks around the house and garden, getting away from my desk and screens.

I find that this helps me to remain calm and relaxed.. and generally happier. I advise you all to do the same.

We also had a family chat on FaceTime last night which was great, lovely to see all the family. It was also great to see my brother, it was his birthday yesterday and rather being able to celebrate, open presents (even get presents), his day was spent in isolation and celebrating on his own. I will probably be doing the same in couple of weeks when my birthday comes around

Make sure you all get out the house for your one exercise a day, remembering social distancing from others, and stop yourself from going mad

LOCKDOWN

Tuesday 24th March

Last night it was announced that the country would be put on lockdown. This put strict guidelines for leaving the house.

  1. Stay at home - No unnecessary journeys or social distancing.
  2. Only leave the house for essential shopping or medical needs
  3. One form of exercise per day (eg. Walk, Run, Cycle)
  4. No travelling to work unless absolutly neccessary
  5. Public gatherings of more than two people are banned (excluding people you live with)
  6. Police can fine you if you don't follow the rules

These guidelines have been a long time coming and really hope that they are adhered to, unfortunately we know that certain few will not and expect that further and more stricter guidelines will be put in place

The death toll is rising, now at 335 deaths. How is this not scaring people to act more responsible?! We should be protecting ourselves, our loved ones and family. It must be tough for families that live in seperate households, thank god for technology like FaceTime and Skype that allows them to keep in touch and talk, definately helping to cope with the cabin fever.

It seems the best thing we can do is to keep our minds active, make use of our gardens while its hot, spring clean the house now so that when able to move more freely then can make the most of it... and relax

Time to self-isolate

Wednesday 18th March

Today is the last day (for now) that I will be working in the office, we have been told to now work from home

It is a new experience, once seen to be a great thing to do, but now, for me, quite lonely.

It is nice to know that I can spend more time at home with Lara and Luna, have lunch with them and be able to just come downstairs when finished for the day. Whilst we shall be saving money with not having to drive anywhere, we shall possibly see that increase in electric.

I have now stopped teaching my exercise classes for now, which means that I won't have any reason to leave the house. My best intention is to ensure that I am walking on my lunch hour so that I get fresh air and exercise.
